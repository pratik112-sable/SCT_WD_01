Nature is the ultimate source of our living. Both living and non-living things include nature, and everyone is interdependent, which helps maintain the ecosystem. Plants, animals, and humans all depend on nature for their survival.It supplies oxygen, sunlight, soil, water, and other necessary components.


It underpins our economy, our society, indeed our very existence. Our forests, rivers, oceans and soils provide us with the food we eat, the air we breathe, the water we irrigate our crops with. We also rely on them for numerous other goods and services we depend on for our health, happiness and prosperity.

About Section




Nature refers to the physical world and the living organisms within it. It encompasses everything from the smallest microorganisms to the largest ecosystems, including plants, animals, mountains, oceans, and the atmosphere. Here are some key aspects of nature:

1. Biodiversity Nature is incredibly diverse, with millions of species of plants, animals, fungi, and microorganisms. This biodiversity is crucial for the health of ecosystems and the services they provide.

2. Ecosystems An ecosystem is a community of living organisms interacting with each other and their environment. Examples include forests, deserts, oceans, and wetlands. Each ecosystem has its own complex network of relationships and processes.

3. Natural Processes Nature operates through various natural processes, such as the water cycle, photosynthesis, and nutrient cycling. These processes are essential for sustaining life and maintaining ecological balance.

4. Conservation Conservation efforts aim to protect and preserve nature and its biodiversity. This includes safeguarding endangered species, restoring damaged ecosystems, and promoting sustainable practices.

5. Human Impact Human activities, such as deforestation, pollution, and climate change, have significant effects on nature. Addressing these impacts is vital for ensuring the long-term health of our planet.

6. Nature's Benefits Nature provides numerous benefits, including:

Ecosystem Services: Such as clean air and water, pollination of crops, and climate regulation. Health and Well-being: Exposure to natural environments can improve mental and physical health. Resources: Nature supplies resources like food, medicine, and raw materials.

7. Connection to Nature Humans have a deep connection to nature, which has inspired art, philosophy, and scientific inquiry throughout history. Spending time in nature can foster a sense of peace and wonder.

Nature is an inherent character or constitution,[1] particularly of the ecosphere or the universe as a whole. In this general sense nature refers to the laws, elements and phenomena of the physical world, including life. Although humans are part of nature, human activity or humans as a whole are often described as at times at odds, or outright separate and even superior to nature

A Nature-based Solution (NBS) is an action that incorporates natural features and processes to protect, conserve, restore, sustainably use, and manage natural or modified ecosystems to address socio-environmental challenges while providing measurable co-benefits to and benefit both people and nature.
